Alekhya

Emotions and culture are the two pillars upon which the colossal edifice of human civilisation is uniquely sculptured. It’s the uniqueness of several cultures and their ethos which have diversified the human race into different factions, beliefs and thinking. Art, in the form of paintings, sketches and sculptures is an imprint of the cultural development of any human civilization. Art is another realm of life; it’s the world of aesthetic and speechless conveyance (called Alekhya in Sanskrit). Language has played a huge role in human evolution. As primates, we started our communication with our fellow beings through the pictorial representation of our feelings and emotions. Therefore, it will not be wrong to say that, art has played a major in our evolution. Gradually we evolved and so did art and its forms.


Nowadays, modern art and westernisation of culture is dominant throughout world. Unknowingly, we have neglected our own culture and it’s glorious history. With the aim of appreciating and reviving Indian Culture and Aestheticism, Alekhya is themed on the popular folk painting traditions from different parts of India.


The event is divided into 4 independent segments of 15 days each. In each segment we are going to give a theme – one specific Indian cultural art form. The participants are required to marry the uniqueness of the art form with modern ideas and come up with something novel and aesthetic.

Awards

The overall front-runner of each segment will be featured on Pravega’s social media handles as well as on a banner during the main fest. In addition, the winner of each phase will be awarded with a cash prize and e-certificates.

Phases and themes

  • 1st to 15th September Madhubani paintings
  • 16th to 30th September Rajasthani paintings
  • 1th to 15th October Pattachitra Paintings
  • 16th to 30th October Tanjore Art

Eligibility

Open to all. Free entry.
